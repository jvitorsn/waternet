\section*{\centering{ABSTRACT}} 
The need for redundant odometry with the most diverse capture sources benefits from the recent possibilities of fast and efficient real-time image processing by means of classifications and regressions. The present work proposes a Keras-based approach by means of Convolutional Neural Networks (CNN) used to numerically detect the distance between the image shutter and the water surface. Our CNN approach was trained initially with synthetic images generated by Blender, and evaluated by real images at heights between 50 and 330 cm. This is an important feature to be used in Drones for height estimation above water. Preliminary tests demonstrated an ability of the system to recognize different distances from the water surface even with models trained with synthetic images, and sparse test bench after proper offset treatment and averaging.

{\bf Key-words: UAV, offshore, machine learning, height estimation}
